**One-off** **Events**

Traditionally, u3a Group meetings take place at regular intervals and
have an ongoing list of members.

Most u3a's will also operate ad-hoc one-off events such as a Coach
Outing. The trip may well be offered to all members, or perhaps a pool
of interested members that will number more than the capacity of the
coach.

One way to manage this is to create a Group that will only have one
scheduled event. It may be a good idea to prefix this with "z" so it
appears out of the way at the bottom of the Groups listing.

In this example the group **zTrip** **to** **London** **next** **year**
is limited to 12 attendees. Once publicised, the Group Leader *Hilary*
*Barker* can add members to the group. This makes it easier to
communicate to those going, manage numbers, and to create a register on
the day.

After the event the Group can be given the status "Inactive" and
eventually deleted.

**IMPORTANT:**

Please note that deleting an Event will not affect transactions relating
to that Event though the transactions will no longer be associated with
that Event. Therefore, it is strongly recommended that the **Details**
box states the name of the Event and the reason for payment so that
subsequent examination of accounts can be fully explained. This **must**
be done before the transaction is reconciled (cleared).

***Tip:***

*An* *alternative* *way* *to* *manage* *one-off* *events* *and*
*payments* *is* *to* *initially* *set* *up* *the* *Event* *with* *a*
*limit* *of* *1* *attendee* *(the* *organiser)* *and* *a* *Waiting*
*List.* *Members* *expressing* *an* *interest* *in* *the* *event* *can*
*be* *added* *to* *the* *Waiting* *List* *by* *the* *organiser,* *or*
*by* *the* *member* *via* *the* *Members* *Portal.*

*Once* *payments* *have* *been* *received,* *members* *can* *be*
*admitted* *to* *the* *Group* *(having* *first* *increased* *the*
*Waiting* *List* *limit)* *and* *associated* *transactions* *can* *be*
*added* *to* *the* *ledger.*

**Regular** **Events**

The techniques described above can also be used for regular events. For
example, a Walking Group in Beacon may well have all the u3a's members
who are interested in walking -many more than can be accommodated on one
walk. Individual walks can be created as temporary Beacon Groups in the
same way as the coach trip example.
