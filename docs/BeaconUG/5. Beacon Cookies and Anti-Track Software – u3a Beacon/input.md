**5.** **Beacon** **Cookies** **and** **Anti-Track** **Software**

> <img src="./lhrhk5pa.png"
> style="width:0.41667in;height:0.41667in" /><img src="./upd24lgz.png"
> style="width:0.15625in;height:0.15625in" />Graham Tigg Follow 1 year
> ago · Updated

If you accept optional cookies with the Cookie Control (see [<u>2.
Logging in as a S</u>y<u>stem
User</u>)](https://u3abeacon.zendesk.com/hc/en-gb/articles/360007072538)
then Beacon will ask your browser to store the personal settings and
choices listed below. Consent lasts for 90 days, after which your
browser will expire the cookies and your settings will be lost.

You can open the Cookie Control (click on the cog in the bottom left) at
any time and click "I Accept Optional Cookies" to extend consent expiry
to 90 days in the future.

If you find your settings have reset before 90 days then then it could
be:

> You have cleared cookies on your browser (this can also happen when
> you clear your cache) Your are using a different browser or computer
>
> You have opened the Cookie Control and clicked "I Do Not Accept
> Cookies".

The settings cookies record are:

> The name of the u3a site you login to
>
> The timeout period of inactivity after which you will be logged out
>
> How forenames and surnames appear and are sorted on Drop-Down lists
> The last membership class selected for exporting addresses and labels
> Settings used when labels were last printed (labels per sheet and
> positioning) The membership Status and Class used for Third Age
> Matters submission
>
> When composing an email, the last selected ‘From’ address and ‘Tick to
> receive copy’ a copy selection The last Financial account used for an
> operation (e.g. add member, renew) will be remembered as the default.

Tip:

If you print address labels then make a note of settings that work for a
particular type of stationary.

**Anti-Track** **Software**<img src="./wjccbyf0.png"
style="width:0.1875in;height:0.18725in" />

There is the possibility that some Anti-track software will prevent
Beacon's cookie banner from working correctly.

Beacon uses optional cookies only to save User preferences. Beacon
cookies are only ever stored on the User's device and are never
uploaded, analysed shared or used for marketing or tracking.

If you accept optional cookies in Beacon will remember your u3a name on
the login page, your printer setings, the last e-mail address you used
to send e-mail etc. The cookies acceptance page gives details of items
saved.

Although Beacon does not use any tracking cookies, some Anti-track
software marks them as such which then causes the cookie banner to
appear at every change of page in Beacon.

If you experience this problem, please mark Beacon or its URL
**u3abeacon.org.uk** as an exception in the Anti-track software.
