# 60. Personal Preferences

The **Personal Preferences** page lets you customise how Beacon2 looks and
behaves for you, change your password, and update your security question. Every
user can access this page -- no special privilege is required.

To open it, click **Personal preferences** in the **Misc** section of the
Home page.

![The Personal Preferences page](images/personal-preferences.png)

---

## Display Preferences

The first section controls how Beacon2 appears on your screen.

1. **Text size** [A] -- choose from **Small**, **Normal**, **Large**, or
   **Extra Large**. The change takes effect immediately, so you can try each
   option and pick the one that is most comfortable for you.

2. **Colour theme** [B] -- choose between **Default** (the standard Beacon2
   colours) and **High Contrast** (bolder colours and stronger outlines, which
   may be easier to read on some screens).

> **Tip:** These settings are stored against your user account, so they will
> apply whichever computer or device you use to log in.

---

## Drop-down Name Lists & Timeout

This section controls how member names are shown in drop-down lists throughout
Beacon2, and how long your session lasts before you are automatically logged
out.

1. **Sort by** [C] -- choose whether name lists are sorted by **Surname** or
   **Forename**.

2. **Display format** [D] -- choose how names appear in lists:
   - **Surname, Forename** (e.g. "Smith, Jane")
   - **Forename Surname** (e.g. "Jane Smith")

3. **Session timeout** [E] -- set the number of minutes of inactivity before
   Beacon2 automatically logs you out. Enter any value between **5** and
   **99** minutes. If you step away from your computer and the timeout period
   passes, you will need to log in again.

> **Tip:** A shorter timeout (e.g. 15 minutes) is more secure, especially if
> you share a computer. A longer timeout is more convenient if you are working
> through a large task.

---

## Change Password

Use this section to update your Beacon2 password at any time.

1. **Current password** [F] -- type your existing password to confirm it is
   really you making the change.

2. **New password** [G] -- type your chosen new password. As you type, a
   **strength bar** appears below the field showing a score from **0** (very
   weak) to **5** (very strong). The bar changes colour from red through amber
   to green as the password gets stronger. Hints are shown beneath the bar to
   help you meet the requirements:
   - Between 10 and 72 characters
   - At least one uppercase letter, one lowercase letter, and one number
   - No spaces

3. **Confirm password** [H] -- type the new password again. A **match
   indicator** will appear to tell you whether the two entries are identical.

4. Press **Change password** to save. You will see a confirmation message if
   the change is successful.

> **Tip:** See [Section 3](3-changing-password.md) for full details on
> password requirements.

---

## Change Personal Q&A

Your security question and answer are used if you ever need to recover your
account (for example, if you forget your password).

1. **Question** [I] -- enter a question whose answer only you would know.
   Maximum **200 characters**.

2. **Answer** [J] -- enter the answer to your question. Maximum **200
   characters**. Remember the exact format you use (including capitalisation),
   because you will need to type it the same way during account recovery.

3. Press **Save** to update your question and answer.
