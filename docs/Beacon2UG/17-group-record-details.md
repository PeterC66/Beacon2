# 17. Group Record -- Details

When you open a group from the Groups List (or create a new group), you arrive
at the **Group Record**. For an existing group this page has several tabs --
**Details**, **Members**, **Schedule**, and **Ledger**. For a new group only
the Details tab is shown until you save.

This section covers the **Details** tab.

![The Group Record -- Details tab](images/17-group-record-details.png)

## Fields on the Details tab

### Group Name (required)

The name of the group [A]. This is the only field you must fill in -- all
other fields are optional. The name appears in the Groups List, the Calendar,
and the Members Portal.

### Faculty

A dropdown [B] listing all faculties (subject categories) set up in your u3a.
Choose one to assign the group to a faculty, or leave it as "none". Faculties
help organise groups by topic (see [Section 22: Faculties](22-faculties.md)).

### Status

A dropdown [C] with two choices: **Active** or **Inactive**. New groups
default to Active. Setting a group to Inactive keeps the record but removes it
from most day-to-day lists. You cannot change this field on a brand-new group
until the record has been saved for the first time.

### When

A free-text field [D] where you describe when the group meets -- for example
"2nd Thursday at 2:00pm" or "Every Monday morning". This text appears in the
Groups List so members can see meeting times at a glance.

### Start time / End time

Two time fields [E] for the group's usual start and end times. These are
optional and are used when generating calendar entries.

### Venue

A dropdown [F] showing all venues from the Venues list (see
[Section 21: Venues](21-venues.md)). Select the venue where the group
normally meets.

### Enquiries

A text field [G] for the name and/or phone number of someone members can
contact with questions about the group.

### Max members

An optional number [H] setting the maximum membership for the group. When
combined with the waiting list feature, members who try to join once the group
is full will be placed on the waiting list instead.

### Checkboxes

Below the main fields are several checkboxes that control how the group
behaves, particularly in the Members Portal:

| Checkbox | What it does |
|----------|--------------|
| **Allow members to join/leave online** [I] | Members can join or leave the group through the Members Portal |
| **Enable waiting list** [J] | When the group is full, new members are placed on a waiting list |
| **Notify leader when members join/leave** [K] | The group leader receives an email whenever a member joins or leaves |
| **Display waiting list by default** [L] | The Members tab shows waiting-list members by default |
| **Show member addresses to group leader** [M] | Group leaders can see member addresses in the Members Portal |

### Information

A multi-line text area [N] for public-facing information about the group.
This text may be displayed on the Members Portal and your website.

### Notes

A multi-line text area [O] for private notes. These are only visible to
Beacon2 administrators and are not shown to members or the public.

> **SiteWorks note:** If **SiteWorks** integration is activated in System
> Settings, the **When**, **Start/End time**, **Venue**, **Enquiries**, and
> **Information** fields are hidden on this tab, as scheduling is managed
> through SiteWorks instead. The Schedule tab is also hidden.

## Saving your changes

Click the **Save Record** button [P] (or **Add Group** when creating a new
group) to save. A green confirmation banner appears at the top of the page
when the save is successful.

If you try to navigate away with unsaved changes, Beacon2 will warn you.

## Deleting a group

If you have the appropriate permission, a **Delete Group** button [Q] appears
next to the Save button. Clicking it asks you to confirm before the group is
permanently removed. Deleting a group also removes all its member
associations, schedule events, and ledger entries.
