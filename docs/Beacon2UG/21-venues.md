# 21. Venues

The Venues page lets you manage the list of places where your u3a's groups
meet and events take place. Venue records are shared across the system --
once you add a venue here, it becomes available in the Group Record and
Calendar.

## Opening the Venues list

From the **Home** page, click **Venues** in the navigation bar. You will see
a table listing all of your u3a's venues.

![The Venues list](images/21-venues.png)

---

## What the list shows

Each row in the table shows:

| Column | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Name** [A] | The name of the venue (click to edit) |
| **Town** [B] | The town where the venue is located |
| **Postcode** [C] | The venue's postcode |
| **Private** [D] | A flag if the address is marked as private (e.g. a member's home) |
| **Accessible** [E] | A flag if the venue is wheelchair accessible |

Click a venue name to open it for editing.

---

## The venue editor

When you click a venue (or click **Add New Venue**), the editor opens with
the following fields:

![The Venue editor](images/21-venue-editor.png)

### Name (required)

The name of the venue [F] -- for example "Village Hall" or "Community
Centre". This is the only field you must fill in.

### Address fields

- **Address line 1** [G]
- **Address line 2** [H]
- **Town** [I]
- **County** [J]
- **Postcode** [K]

Fill in as much of the address as you have. The postcode is particularly
useful because it enables the map links (see below).

### Contact details

- **Telephone** [L] -- the venue's phone number
- **Email** [M] -- the venue's email address
- **Website** [N] -- the venue's website URL

### Notes

A multi-line text area [O] for any additional information about the venue --
for example parking arrangements, access instructions, or room names.

### Checkboxes

| Checkbox | What it does |
|----------|--------------|
| **Private address** [P] | Marks the venue as a private address (e.g. someone's home). Private venues may be hidden from public listings |
| **Wheelchair accessible** [Q] | Indicates the venue has wheelchair access. This information can be shown to members |

---

## Map links

If a venue has a postcode, two external links appear on the venue record:

- **Streetmap** [R] -- opens the location on Streetmap.co.uk
- **Google Maps** [S] -- opens the location in Google Maps

These are handy for checking the location or sharing directions with members.

---

## Saving your changes

Click **Save** [T] to save the venue record. A confirmation message appears
when the save is successful.

## Deleting a venue

Click **Delete** [U] to remove the venue. You will be asked to confirm
before it is permanently deleted.

> **Note:** If any groups currently use this venue, you may want to reassign
> them to a different venue first.

---

## Adding a new venue

Click **Add New Venue** from the Venues list page to open a blank venue
editor. Fill in the details and click **Save** to create the record.
