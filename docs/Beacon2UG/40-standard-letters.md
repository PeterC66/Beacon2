# 40. Standard Letters

Standard letters are reusable templates that save you from formatting the same
letter over and over again. They work in the same way as standard email messages,
but for the letter compose page.

![Standard letters on the compose page](images/40-standard-letters.png)

---

## Where to find them

Standard letters are managed from the **letter compose page** (see
[Section 39: Composing Letters](39-composing-letters.md)). The Standard Letters
bar sits between the Recipients area and the letter body editor.

---

## Loading a saved template

1. Click the **Load standard letter** dropdown [A].
2. Choose the template you want.
3. The **letter body** is filled in with the saved content, including any
   formatting and personalisation tokens.

You can edit the letter freely after loading -- the original template is not
changed.

---

## Saving a new template

1. Compose your letter body as you would for a normal letter, including any
   formatting and tokens.
2. Click **Save as standard letter** [B]. A name field appears.
3. Type a name for the template (e.g. "Renewal reminder letter" or "Welcome
   pack cover letter").
4. Click **Save**.

The template is now available in the dropdown for all users. If you save with the
same name as an existing template, the old version is replaced.

> **Note:** Saving standard letters requires the appropriate permission. If you
> do not see the **Save as standard letter** button, your role does not include
> that privilege -- ask your administrator.

---

## Deleting a template

To remove a template you no longer need:

1. Select the template from the **Load standard letter** dropdown so you can
   confirm it is the right one.
2. Click **Delete standard letter** [C].
3. Confirm the deletion when prompted.

> **Note:** Deleting standard letters requires the appropriate permission. If you
> do not see the **Delete standard letter** button, your role does not include
> that privilege.

> **Warning:** Deleted templates cannot be recovered. If you think you might need
> it again, consider keeping it or copying the text elsewhere first.

---

## Tips

- Templates can include **personalisation tokens** (e.g. `#FAM`, `#ADDRESSV`).
  When the PDF is generated, these tokens are replaced with each member's
  details -- just like a normal letter.
- Templates also preserve **formatting** -- bold, italic, underline, alignment,
  and font sizes are all saved as part of the template.
- Give your templates clear, descriptive names so that other users can find and
  use them easily.
- Templates are shared across all users of your u3a -- there are no private
  templates.
